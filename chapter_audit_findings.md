## Needs correction before publication

**1. Ch.8 ("The Breaking Point") — factual contradiction with the primary-source police record.**
Current text: "We had been separated for years... there had been no encounter that could even be misinterpreted."
Problem: Det. Holladay's actual investigative file has BOTH Reardon and Crowder agreeing an encounter occurred on Nov 25, 2018 (planned via text messages) — the dispute was only over consent, not whether anything happened. The investigation that CLEARED Reardon rested on this being a mutually-planned, consensual encounter — not on no encounter existing at all.
Fix: Rewrite to "a consensual encounter, confirmed by both parties' own text messages, that she later falsely reported as assault" — this is actually the stronger, more defensible version of the story.

**2. Ch.9 ("The Battle for Evidence") — wrong deputy name.**
Names "Omar Ahmed" as the second deputy present at the Dec 28, 2020 arrest. Every primary-source record points to **Deputy Ethan Tidwell**. Recommend correcting unless a primary document turns up showing both were present.

**3. Ch.15 — case citation error.**
Cites "Davidson v. O'Connor" as the landmark SCOTUS case on involuntary commitment. This case name does not exist in that form. The real, correct case is **O'Connor v. Donaldson** (1975) — already used correctly elsewhere in Reardon's own primary-source communications. Easy fix, but exactly the kind of error a hostile reviewer/opposing counsel would flag instantly.

**4. Ch.13 ("Chancery Court Chess Game") — needs a source check.**
States attorney Christi McCoy is "of the Earlene Gardner Victims Assistance Project" and used DOJ victims-fund resources to represent Crowder. Our confirmed primary-source timeline only documents McCoy as "Crowder's attorney in the Olive Branch matter" with no Earlene Gardner tie. Meanwhile Denise Fondren (Reardon's own conflicted PD) is the one independently confirmed to have Earlene Gardner ties. Either this is a genuinely bigger conflict-of-interest story (the same program touching both sides) or the manuscript is conflating two attorneys. Needs verification against a primary document (bar record/firm affiliation) before publication.

**5. Still outstanding from Ch.6 ("The Ambush"):**
The manuscript frames the Todd/Ashley Lynch aggravated-stalking charge as "fabricated from whole cloth" based solely on the AR-15 purchase-date alibi (bought May 20, allegedly used May 8). But the primary-source timeline shows Investigator Jarrett Bundren reviewed real Facebook posts/videos of Reardon with guns making threatening statements on May 24, 2017 — and that review is what triggered Lynch's complaint, not just the specific date claim. The alibi may still hold for the exact weapon/date alleged, but "fabricated from whole cloth" overstates it. Recommend softening the framing to acknowledge the FB-content trigger while still making the case that the specific charged conduct/date doesn't hold up.
