# U.S. Senator Cindy Hyde-Smith's Office — Two Calls, Aug 2021 (Recordings/Call Recorder folder)

**Files:** `Unknown-To-(202)224-5054-2913217-2021-08-13-14-10-10.m4a` and `Unknown-To-(202)224-5054-7501900-2021-08-23-10-47-27.m4a`. Phone number 202-224-5054 is a U.S. Senate office line. The phone's built-in auto-transcript badly mangled the senator's name as "Senator Hudson" — re-transcribed directly by the agent using Whisper for accurate content.

## Call 1 — Aug 13, 2021
Staffer: "Senator Hudson's office, this is the office, how may I help you?" (garbled — actually Hyde-Smith's office)
Reardon: "I was trying to relay something to the Senator. I'm a constituent, well I live in Bruce now, but I'm from Oxford. It's a serious grievance... one that I believe rises to the level of national security. It's dealing with the Department of Justice. Back in May of 2017, I was framed for a crime that was literally an impossibility for me to have committed." Call drops/garbles before completion.

## Call 2 — Aug 23, 2021 (follow-up, ~10 days later)
Staffer confirms the message was relayed: "I passed the [message] to the Senator and she said that she's not going to be able to do much, and that Governor Reeves would probably be your best bet for any help regarding that matter."

Reardon's response — **the clearest, most specific on-record articulation of the judge-shopping allegation found anywhere in the audio archive, and it predates the Dec 6, 2021 Busby/Luther case-reassignment discovery by nearly 4 months:**

> "The circuit court judge that has literally been in the way on everything, refused me an evidentiary hearing. He was of knowledge of the crimes committed by Lafayette County officials and still has chosen to not recuse himself. I've got nine charges against me in justice court. All the judges in Lafayette County have recused themselves. Now the Union County judge has recused himself, which... is strikingly odd right there that all these judges are recusing, and come to find out the judge that is actually picking the judges to rule over my justice court cases is the same circuit court judge that I feel like is criminally liable. And what a coincidence that [he's] absconding with the liability by picking a favorable judge to get rid of his problem."

Reardon then references U.S. Rep. Matt Gaetz favorably as a due-process advocate, contrasting him with Hyde-Smith's inaction: "I see Matt Gates [sic] down in Florida... certainly taking an interest in due process rights... I don't know why my own state senator, who's a Republican, is refusing to even assist... every single person's turned their back on me just to let me just drown out here."

Staffer: "I'm sorry, sir, that's all I'm going to be able to tell you at this time. I wish I had a better answer for you."

## Why this matters for the manuscript
1. **A federal-senator-level stonewall**, parallel to the state-senator Chris McDaniel stonewall already logged from Voice Memos — both fit a "wrote to everyone, everyone turned him away" passage.
2. **Timing is significant:** Reardon was already articulating the judge-shopping/case-assignment-manipulation theory in August 2021 — well before the Dec 6, 2021 incident (Busby getting defensive, the Kent Smith → Luther reassignment discovery) that's currently treated as the origin point of that allegation in the manuscript timeline. This suggests either (a) Reardon had independent, earlier reason to suspect this pattern, or (b) he was already prone to this interpretation before concrete evidence — worth noting both possibilities fairly.
3. Confirms the specific number "nine charges against me in justice court" from Reardon's own mouth, contemporaneously — useful as a precise, dated citation rather than a vague later claim.
