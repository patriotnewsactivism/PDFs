**2. The habeas-timing / illegal-confinement argument — deliberately held back:**
- Reardon reveals: Kristian McRay's habeas corpus petition was **filed June 5** and **approved to proceed in forma pauperis the very next day, June 6** — but Reardon says he was never brought before a judge as a result, and only found out the writ had even been granted *after* he was released from jail on **July 6**.
- He argues this shows he was illegally confined beyond any legitimate process, and wants to raise it.
- **Andy's explicit advice: do NOT put this in the current PCR pleading.** "Hold on to that... you don't want to show them your complete hand." The tactical reasoning: notice pleading only requires enough to give the state notice of the claim category — front-loading specific dramatic facts limits Reardon later and risks tipping his full argument before an evidentiary hearing.

**3. The "abuse of process" defense — a key legal nuance clarified on the call:**
- Reardon is worried the State's likely defense to his PCR motion is "abuse of process" — the doctrine that a defendant who **knowingly sat on information** before pleading guilty forfeits the right to raise it post-conviction.
- Reardon acknowledges he "sat on the information for three years" — but the actual timeline nuance, confirmed here for the first time: **he didn't have confirmed knowledge of the exculpatory purchase-date evidence at the time of his guilty plea.** He says he'd been "running his mouth" in recorded jail phone calls about buying the gun on May 20th, but the *evidentiary proof* (a bill of sale, a witness named Larry) wasn't something he could act on until later.
- Andy's assessment: "You had a judge that set a bar way too high for what you had to do for the crime you were charged with, and they pressured you, and you weren't going to get out of jail" — i.e., the plea was coerced by jail conditions, not a knowing waiver, which undercuts an abuse-of-process defense IF argued correctly (again: don't over-plead the theory, just state the underlying facts).
